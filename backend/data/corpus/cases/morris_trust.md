Morris Trust remains a doctrinally important case when a divisive transaction is being evaluated alongside a later sale or business combination. It matters because it sits close to the line where a separation is respected as its own transaction rather than collapsed into a taxable sale-oriented plan.

For modern transactional analysis, the case is not a substitute for the statute and regulations. It is useful as a secondary authority that helps explain why sequencing, business purpose, and the relationship between the separation and later sale steps can materially change the strength of a section 355 path.
