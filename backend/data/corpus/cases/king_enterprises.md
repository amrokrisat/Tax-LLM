King Enterprises is a commonly cited step-transaction case in acquisitive reorganization analysis. It is useful when a transaction unfolds in multiple related steps, such as stock purchases followed by mergers or contributions, and the tax analysis depends on whether the steps should be integrated.

For a transactional tax agent, this case supports issue spotting around whether the legal sequence can be viewed separately or whether the overall plan should be tested as one combined transaction. That matters directly in reorganization qualification, contribution planning, and stock-versus-asset comparison work.
