King Enterprises remains useful where the wedge overlaps with reorganization-sensitive sequencing rather than a clean taxable stock acquisition. It helps evaluate whether formally separate steps should be integrated when the parties are comparing a merger path against taxable stock or asset alternatives. For this wedge, it is best used as a narrow overlap authority rather than the main driver of the stock-versus-asset comparison.
