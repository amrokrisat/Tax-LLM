Gregory v. Helvering remains foundational for the business purpose and substance orientation of corporate reorganization analysis. Although modern statutory and regulatory frameworks are more detailed, Gregory still matters whenever a transaction path appears engineered primarily for tax positioning rather than supported by meaningful business objectives.

For practical deal work, Gregory is less about reciting doctrine and more about risk framing. When alternative structures are compared, a path that technically resembles a qualifying reorganization or contribution may still carry meaningful challenge risk if it lacks credible non-tax business reasons, uses transitory entities, or is inconsistent with the actual integration plan.
