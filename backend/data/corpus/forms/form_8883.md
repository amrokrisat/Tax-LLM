Form 8883 is important in the deemed-asset-election wedge because it translates Section 338 modeling into a concrete allocation statement. It helps the team test whether the election path is operationally coherent, whether the deemed sale and new-basis consequences have actually been modeled, and whether the allocation economics justify the election. It is therefore a practical companion to the Code and regulations rather than a substitute for them.
