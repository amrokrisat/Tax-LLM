Form 8883 and its instructions are practically relevant whenever a Section 338 election is being modeled or documented. They reflect the reporting mechanics around old target and new target asset allocations and can help align election modeling with the actual work papers produced by the transaction team.

Like Form 8594, Form 8883 is not a substitute for primary authority. Its value is operational: it helps expose whether a deal team has actually thought through the deemed sale and allocation mechanics implied by the Section 338 election analysis.
