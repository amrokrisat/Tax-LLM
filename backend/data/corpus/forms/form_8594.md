The instructions for Form 8594 matter because they operationalize the reporting side of Section 1060 allocations. In real transaction work, they are often used as a back-end consistency check on whether buyer and seller allocation schedules actually reflect an applicable asset acquisition and whether the parties are aligned on asset-class treatment.

The form is not substantive primary authority in the same way as the Code and regulations, but it is highly practical. It supports diligence and drafting because inconsistencies between the economics memo, purchase agreement allocation exhibit, and Form 8594 reporting can create downstream controversy.
