The Section 382 regulations provide the practical framework for testing ownership changes, identifying five-percent shareholders, and computing the mechanics that determine whether and how limitations apply. In live transactions, these rules are often more important than the statute alone because they determine how equity movements are measured across the testing period and whether complex preferred or derivative arrangements affect the analysis.

This is essential authority for attribute-preservation diligence. A tax agent using Section 382 for issue spotting should not stop at “target has NOLs”; it should ask whether there have been prior ownership shifts, whether the current transaction interacts with historic shifts, and whether equity instruments or restructurings complicate the shareholder measurements under the regulations.
