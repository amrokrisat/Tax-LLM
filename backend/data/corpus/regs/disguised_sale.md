The disguised sale regulations address transfers to partnerships and related distributions, including timing, funding, and liability interactions that can reshape transaction consequences. They are core authority whenever sellers are rolling into a partnership or LLC taxed as a partnership and the economics risk looking more like a sale than a true continuing equity position.

In structure-comparison work, these regulations serve as a practical brake on overly simplistic “just roll into a partnership” planning. They support issue spotting around reimbursement arrangements, leveraged distributions, short-interval transfers and distributions, and other patterns that can transform a purported tax-deferred contribution into current sale treatment.
