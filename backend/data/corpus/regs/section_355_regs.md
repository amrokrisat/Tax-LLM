Regulation 1.355-1 is the operative regulatory framework for evaluating whether a divisive transaction can stay inside section 355. It matters because it gives structure to the practical questions that decide real transactions: whether the distribution is being used as a device, whether the controlled-corporation posture is real, whether the active-trade-or-business requirement can be supported, and whether the larger deal sequence still fits a nonrecognition separation rather than a taxable pre-sale reshuffling.

In practice, this regulation is most important when a separation step is being used to isolate a line of business, park liabilities, or prepare a business for sale. The tax analysis turns on whether the separation has its own supportable corporate logic and regulatory footing, not merely on whether section 355 is mentioned in the deal documents.
