Treas. Reg. Section 1.1060-1 supplies the implementation framework for Section 1060 and the residual method. It is particularly important when translating negotiated economics into tax allocations across asset classes. In practice, it supports the mechanics of purchase-price allocation schedules, disputes over intangible value, and consistency between tax modeling and reporting.

For a transactional tax agent, the regulation matters because it connects structure choice to practical downstream consequences. If the transaction is an applicable asset acquisition, the residual method framework will shape buyer basis, seller gain character, and the reporting expectations that follow. That makes the regulation a core retrieval target when the system compares taxable asset paths against stock or deemed asset paths.
