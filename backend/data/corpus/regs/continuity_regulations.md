Treas. Reg. 1.368-1 provides the practical operating rules for the reorganization statutes. It is the main regulatory authority for continuity of interest, continuity of business enterprise, plan-of-reorganization framing, and the business purpose requirement. For transactional planning, this regulation is usually more useful than the bare statute when testing whether a proposed merger or rollover arrangement is likely to support reorganization treatment.

The regulation matters whenever the deal includes mixed consideration, rollover equity, post-closing integration, or multiple related steps. Continuity of interest issues frequently arise when the parties are close to the line on cash versus equity mix, when rollover equity differs materially from buyer common equity, or when side arrangements undermine the economic reality of the retained equity stake. Continuity of business enterprise issues often surface when the buyer intends to dispose of target assets, liquidate operating lines, or migrate functions immediately after closing. The business purpose discussion is also important in alternative comparisons because a technically eligible form may still be vulnerable if the structure exists mainly to chase a tax result with weak non-tax support.
