The Section 338 regulations provide the operational rules for qualified stock purchases, election mechanics, aggregate deemed sale price, adjusted grossed-up basis, and special election variants including Section 338(h)(10). For deal work, these regulations are where the modeling details live. They guide whether an election is available, how deemed sale and basis consequences are computed, and how the consequences differ between buyer and seller depending on the target’s tax posture and ownership.

These regulations are also central to stock-versus-asset comparison analysis. A nominal stock transaction can be evaluated against asset-style economics by modeling the deemed sale and new-basis consequences under the regulations. They therefore support both threshold issue spotting and the more detailed alternative-comparison work that follows once election availability and party economics are understood.
