Regulation 1.355-2 is the central device and business-purpose authority for deciding whether a separation can remain within section 355 when it sits next to a sale, contribution, financing step, or broader restructuring. It matters because many live transaction questions are not about whether section 355 exists in theory, but whether the distribution has its own supportable corporate purpose and whether the sequence looks like a real separation rather than a sale-preparatory device.

For transactional work, this regulation is the rule set that forces discipline around sequencing, prearranged sale sensitivity, and the difference between a supportable divisive transaction and a taxable reshuffling of businesses or assets before exit.
