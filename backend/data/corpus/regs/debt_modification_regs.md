Treas. Reg. 1.1001-3 supplies the main framework for determining whether changes to debt terms create a deemed exchange through a significant modification. In acquisition planning, that matters whenever the step plan includes post-closing refinancing, amendments to seller notes, maturity extensions, yield changes, or shifts in obligor structure.

This regulation strengthens financing analysis by moving beyond the simple question whether interest is deductible. It helps the agent ask whether refinancing steps could trigger exchange consequences, whether new issue price concepts matter, and whether the selected structure creates avoidable tax friction when debt is revised soon after signing or closing.
