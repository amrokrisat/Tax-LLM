Regulation 1.338(h)(10)-1 is the operational source for one of the most realistic election alternatives in the wedge. It matters because it ties the buyer's desire for asset-style basis results to concrete ownership, election, and seller-consent requirements. In practice, this regulation often determines whether a stock deal can be re-priced into a deemed asset result, whether a seller gross-up conversation is unavoidable, and whether the election path is truly available rather than merely tax-efficient in theory.
