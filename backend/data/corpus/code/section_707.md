Section 707 is the core statutory authority for transactions between a partner and a partnership, including disguised sale analysis. It matters whenever a proposed rollover, contribution, reimbursement, or distribution starts to look less like continuing equity and more like a sale with partnership-form wrapping.

For transactional planning, section 707 is central when sellers contribute property or equity to a partnership or LLC taxed as a partnership while also receiving cash, debt relief, or related distributions. It is the statute that forces the analysis to test whether the structure is really tax-deferred or whether sale treatment is being triggered by the surrounding economics.
