Section 382 is the core attribute-preservation authority for the wedge when the buyer is comparing stock form against asset or deemed asset alternatives. A straight stock acquisition may preserve the entity and its history, but that does not mean the target's losses or other attributes remain fully usable. Section 382 therefore sits directly inside the buyer-side tradeoff: if the buyer values inherited attributes, the ownership-change limitations must be modeled alongside any basis-step-up alternative. If those attributes are already limited or fragile, a stock path can look less attractive relative to an asset-style result.
