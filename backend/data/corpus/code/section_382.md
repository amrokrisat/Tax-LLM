Section 382 is the principal statutory constraint on post-acquisition use of pre-change net operating losses and certain other tax attributes. It applies when an ownership change occurs, generally through significant shifts in ownership by five-percent shareholders over the testing period. In transactional planning, Section 382 is not just a tax-attribute afterthought; it can materially influence purchase price, structural choice, financing design, and whether preserving target-level history is worth the complexity of a stock or reorganization path.

Practically, Section 382 analysis starts with issue spotting rather than final modeling. Teams need to determine whether the target actually has meaningful NOLs or built-in items, whether historic cap table movements already consumed headroom, whether the contemplated transaction creates an ownership change, and whether debt conversions, rollover equity, PIPE-like pre-closing investments, or internal restructurings affect the shareholder testing story. For a transactional tax agent, Section 382 is therefore a core bucket not only for citation support, but also for missing-fact generation because attribute preservation often depends on facts that do not appear cleanly in initial deal summaries.
