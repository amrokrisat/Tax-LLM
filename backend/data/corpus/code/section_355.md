Section 355 is the core statutory framework for divisive corporate transactions, including spin-offs, split-offs, and split-ups. It matters whenever a seller is considering a separation step before a sale, contribution, or acquisition because the viability of that step depends on controlled-corporation mechanics, active-trade-or-business requirements, device sensitivity, and the overall distribution posture.

For transactional work, section 355 is not just a background restructuring rule. It can materially change which assets, liabilities, and businesses sit in the sold corporation, whether a later sale occurs after a supportable separation or after a taxable shuffle, and whether the parties can isolate unwanted assets or liabilities without collapsing the structure into a taxable event.
