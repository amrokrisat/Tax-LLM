Sections 354, 356, 361, and 368 supply the core statutory architecture for acquisitive reorganizations. Section 368 defines the statutory forms, including forward and reverse triangular mergers and stock-for-stock or stock-for-assets exchanges. Section 354 generally protects exchanging shareholders from current gain or loss when qualifying stock or securities are received pursuant to the plan of reorganization. Section 356 addresses the boot problem and is the first stop whenever the transaction includes cash, debt reduction, earnouts, preferred equity, or other nonqualifying property. Section 361 addresses the transferor corporation side, including when gain is not recognized on transfers of property to the acquiring corporation or distributions made under the plan.

For transactional planning, these provisions frame several recurring workstreams. First, the consideration package must be tested to determine whether the equity component is sufficient to preserve continuity-sensitive treatment and whether boot produces partial recognition. Second, the actual step plan matters: contribution steps, pre-closing recapitalizations, debt refinancing, and post-closing mergers can all affect whether the statutory form and integrated transaction still fit within Section 368. Third, where rollover equity is used, practitioners need to analyze whether the rollover instrument is truly stock or securities for these purposes and whether governance or liquidity terms undermine the intended continuity position. These sections are therefore central not only to reorganization qualification, but also to alternative-comparison work whenever the parties are choosing between a taxable stock sale, a reorganization path, or a deemed asset alternative.
