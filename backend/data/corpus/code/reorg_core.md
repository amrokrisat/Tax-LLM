Sections 354, 356, 361, and 368 remain relevant to the stock-versus-asset wedge because rollover equity and mixed consideration can move a transaction out of a simple taxable comparison and into reorganization-sensitive territory. These provisions are important when the parties want some stock-form continuity and are comparing that path against either a taxable stock acquisition or an asset-style structure. In this wedge, they help answer whether the retained equity is meaningful enough to support continuity-sensitive treatment and whether boot or sequencing changes the comparison.
