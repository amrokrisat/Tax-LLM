Section 351 is the principal nonrecognition rule for contribution transactions in the corporate setting. It protects transfers of property to a corporation if the transferors, as a group, control the corporation immediately after the exchange. For transactional work, it becomes relevant in pre-acquisition drop-downs, blocker formations, rollover structures in which sellers contribute equity into a holdco, and internal restructuring steps used to prepare a target for sale.

The practical issues are rarely limited to the bare statutory test. Planners need to identify who counts in the transferor group, whether services are being mixed with property, whether debt or preferred instruments create disguised consideration issues, and whether any side arrangements cause control to be too transitory. Section 351 is also central when comparing a straightforward taxable sale to a contribution-plus-sale structure because basis carryover, holding periods, and built-in gain location can all differ materially depending on whether the contribution step is respected.
