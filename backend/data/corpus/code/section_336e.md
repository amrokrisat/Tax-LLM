Section 336(e) is the statutory foundation for treating certain stock sales or distributions as asset transfers under regulations prescribed by the Secretary. It matters because it creates a real competing election path to section 338 in some stock-form deals, but only when the seller-target profile fits the qualified-stock-disposition rules and the parties can satisfy the election mechanics that make the deemed asset consequences operative rather than merely attractive in theory.
