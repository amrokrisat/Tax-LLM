Section 384 matters where the stock-versus-asset decision turns in part on how preacquisition losses can or cannot shelter later gain. It gives the wedge more nuance than a simple Section 382 flag because the question is not only whether attributes survive, but whether they can be used against the income streams the buyer expects after closing. That makes Section 384 particularly relevant when the buyer is considering keeping stock form but also expects integration steps, asset sales, or other post-acquisition gain events.
